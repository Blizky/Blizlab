Blizlab Help & FAQ

Quick answers about import, cutout, chroma key cleanup, layers, and exports. This page is practical on purpose: what each button does, what is destructive, and where limits are.

---

How do I add an image?

  • Click Image to upload from file.
  • Drag and drop directly on the canvas area.
  • Click Paste or use Ctrl/Cmd + V.

In Layers mode, imported files are added as new layers. In Retro and BGone, new imports replace the current source image.

---

What formats are accepted?

Blizlab accepts browser-decoded image/* types (JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and others your browser supports).

⚠️ Exact format support depends on the browser. If your browser cannot decode the file, import fails.

Large images are constrained to 4096px on the longest side to keep editing responsive.

---

Why did my image resize or look softer?

If Upscale low-res images is enabled, Blizlab may stretch small sources to fit the working canvas and selected definition (SD / HD / 4K).

⚠️ Upscale is simple stretch upscale (not AI super-resolution). Expect softness or pixelation on very small inputs.
---

How to cut out a subject (AI)

  • Open BGone mode.
  • Click Cutout.
  • Use Border slider and brush tools Erase/Restore to refine.
  • Use Undo/Redo while refining.
⚠️ First use downloads the U²-Net model once. After that, model runs offline in that browser profile.

Useful shortcuts in BGone: E erase, R restore, Z undo, Y redo, hold Space to pan.

---

How to remove chroma key color

In BGone, click Remove chroma key. It is tuned for green-screen style keys and includes despill logic, then you can refine with brush tools.

⚠️ Green-biased by design. Non-green keys may require more manual cleanup.
---

Layers limits and destructive behavior

  • Maximum layers: 5.
  • If limit is reached, add/duplicate actions are blocked until you delete one.
⚠️ To keep the workflow simple and fast, some actions are destructive.
Background tools on a layer (cutout/chroma from the layer tool) apply changes directly to that layer image.
Delete removes a layer immediately.
Best practice: duplicate a layer first before heavy edits.
---

How to create GIF animations

  • Need at least 2 layers.
  • Open Layers mode and use Create Parallax Animation.
  • Tune duration, intensity, quality preset, FPS, loop mode, and motion type.
  • Render opens a preview. Save when it looks right.
⚠️ Longer duration, higher FPS, and higher quality increase render time and file size.

Common confusion points

  • "Download" in Layers mode exports merged composition PNG, not separate files.
  • ZIP export is where you get each layer as individual PNG.
  • Solo mode temporarily mutes other layers until solo is toggled off.
  • Canvas definition changes working/export scale and can affect speed.
